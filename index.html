    Table Cheat Sheet

    Print one copy per player and keep it at the rack for your first few games.

    The Set: 152 Tiles

    108 suits (Craks 1-9 × 4, Bams 1-9 × 4, Dots 1-9 × 4) · 16 Winds (N, E, S, W × 4) · 12 Dragons (Red, Green, White × 4) · 8 Flowers · 8 Jokers

    Charleston (ROLLOR)

    First Charleston (mandatory): Right → Over → Left
    Second Charleston (optional, can be stopped after first Left): Left → Over → Right
    Courtesy Pass (optional): 1-3 tiles across, if both agree.
    Blind passes allowed only on the third pass of each Charleston (1st Left, 2nd Right).

    You CAN do these with Jokers

    • Use in a pung (3 of a kind)
    • Use in a kong (4 of a kind)
    • Use in a quint (5 of a kind)
    • Use in a sextet (6 of a kind)
    • Joker Exchange: swap your natural tile for an opponent's joker in their exposed set

    You CANNOT do these with Jokers

    • Never in a pair
    • Never in a single
    • Never in NEWS sequences
    • Never pass a joker in the Charleston
    • Never claim a discarded joker (it's dead)

    A Turn

    1. Draw from the wall (skip on East's first turn)
    2. Decide: Mah Jongg? Or which tile to discard?
    3. Discard — place it face-up and name it out loud

    Claiming a Discard

    • Call "Take it!" before the next player draws
    • You must already have 2+ matching tiles (for pung/kong/quint) — OR it must complete Mah Jongg
    • You expose the set face-up on your rack and discard to continue
    • Pairs cannot be claimed except as the final Mah Jongg tile

    The Most-Forgotten Rules

    • 1 Bam looks like a bird, not a bamboo
    • White Dragon = "Soap" = also the "0" in 2026 hands
    • Pairs cannot use jokers
    • NEWS sequences cannot use jokers
    • Same color on card = same suit; different colors = different suits
    • Play goes counter-clockwise
    • Hand has 14 tiles total — count them when verifying a Mah Jongg call
